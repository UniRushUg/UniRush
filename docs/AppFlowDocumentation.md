## **1. Platform Overview** 

UniRush  is a campus-focused delivery application that connects individuals who need items delivered with available delivery personnel — including boda boda riders, bicycle couriers, and fellow students. The platform streamlines on-campus and near-campus deliveries, offering a fast, reliable, and affordable service for the university community. 

|**User Type**|**Who They Are**|**What They Do**|**Key Identifier**|
|---|---|---|---|
|Client|Student / buyer|Places orders, requests<br>transport|Student ID|
|Seller|Restaurant,<br>supermarket, shop|Lists products, fulfills orders|Business TIN|
|Boda Rider|Anyone with a<br>motorcycle|Picks up & delivers orders|Permit or Student<br>ID|
|Car Owner|Anyone with a car|Transports passengers or<br>bulk goods|DL + logbook|
|Student Agent|Student with own<br>vehicle|Earns by delivering while<br>studying|Reg. no. + access<br>no.|



_Note: A Student Agent is a student who has activated the Boda or Car role on their student account. They operate under a lighter verification track and a specific set of policies described in Section 3._ 

## **2. Sign Up & Login** 

Every user goes through a unified registration gateway before diverging into their role-specific onboarding. The app is available on Android, iOS, and the web at unirush.ug. 

## **2.1 Common Registration Steps (All Users)** 

**Step 1: Download the App —** Available on Android (Play Store), iOS (App Store), and at web.unirush.ug. 

**Step 2: Select Your Role —** Client, Seller, Boda Rider, Car Owner, or Student Agent. A student can select multiple roles and manage them under one account. 

**Step 3: Enter Phone Number —** Phone number is the primary account identifier. A 6-digit OTP is sent via SMS to verify ownership. 

**Step 4: Create a Password —** Minimum 8 characters including at least one number. Biometric login can be enabled after first sign-in. 

**Step 5: Upload Profile Photo —** A clear, recent face photo is required for all users. Riders and drivers must ensure their photo matches their ID. 

**Step 6: Role-Specific Verification —** Additional documents are required depending on role (see sections below). 

**Step 7: Account Review & Approval —** Clients and Student Agents are approved within minutes. Sellers and professional riders are reviewed within 24–48 hours. 

**Step 8: Login —** Subsequent logins use phone + password or biometrics. Users with multiple roles see a Role Switcher on the home screen. 

## **2.2 Client (Student) Registration** 

Clients complete the fastest onboarding — designed for immediate access. 

- University or institution (selected from dropdown) 

- Student registration number and access number 

- Hostel or campus delivery zone (updateable per order) 

- Mobile money number (MTN MoMo or Airtel Money) 

_Note: Student ID is automatically verified for partner universities. For others, a photo of the student ID card is uploaded and reviewed within 2 hours._ 

## **2.3 Seller Registration** 

- Business name and type (restaurant, supermarket, boutique, pharmacy, etc.) 

- Physical location — GPS pin and written address 

- Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) Tax Identification Number (TIN) 

- KCCA or local authority trading licence 

- Business phone number and email 

- Operating hours and service radius 

- At least 3 photos of the physical premises 

- Bank account or mobile money number for payouts 

- Food sellers: valid health/hygiene certificate from Ministry of Health or KCCA 

## **2.4 Professional Boda Rider Registration** 

For riders who are not students, or students with a full permit: 

- National Identification Number (NIN) 

- Valid motorcycle riding permit (Class A or B) 

- Registered motorcycle number plate 

- Log book or proof of ownership / employer letter 

- Police clearance certificate (not older than 6 months) 

- Emergency contact — name and phone number 

- Mobile money account for earnings 

## **2.5 Professional Car Owner / Driver Registration** 

- National Identification Number (NIN) 

- Valid driving permit (Class BM or higher) 

- Vehicle number plate and log book 

- Valid third-party insurance certificate 

- Certificate of fitness (roadworthiness) 

- Police clearance certificate (not older than 6 months) 

- Vehicle photos: front, rear, interior, dashboard 

- Mobile money account for earnings 

_Note: Car owners must re-submit insurance and certificate of fitness annually to keep their account active._ 

## **3. Student Agent — Dual Role (Student + Rider/Driver)** 

UniRush explicitly supports students who own motorcycles or cars and wish to earn income through the platform without undergoing the full professional rider onboarding. This is called the Student Agent track. 

The Student Agent track is designed to be inclusive and low-barrier while still maintaining minimum safety and accountability standards. It is governed by the Student Agent Policy which the student must read and accept before activation. 

## **3.1 Who Qualifies as a Student Agent** 

- Currently enrolled student at any recognised Ugandan university or tertiary institution 

- Owns or has permission to use a motorcycle or car 

- Has an active UniRush Client account (student must be registered as a Client first) 

- Is at least 18 years old 

## **3.2 Student Agent — Motorcycle Track** 

A student with a motorcycle who does not yet hold a formal riding permit can register under the Motorcycle Student Agent track using the following: 

- Student registration number (e.g. S25B13/129) 

- Student access number (e.g. B36048) 

- University name and current academic year 

- Motorcycle number plate (must be a registered plate — unplated motorcycles are not accepted) 

- Photo of the motorcycle (front and side) 

- Emergency contact — name and phone number 

- Declaration that they are the owner or have the owner's permission to use the vehicle 

⚠ Students without a riding permit are restricted to on-campus and peri-campus deliveries only (within 3 km of the university gate). They are not permitted to accept inter-campus or longdistance jobs. This restriction is automatically enforced by the platform's geofencing system. 

Students who later obtain a formal riding permit can upload it to upgrade to the full Professional Boda Rider tier, removing the distance restriction. 

## **3.3 Student Agent — Car Track** 

A student who owns or has access to a car registers under the Car Student Agent track: 

- Student registration number and access number 

- University name and current year 

- Valid driving permit (Class BM or higher) — a permit is required for car operations even on the Student Agent track, as it is a legal requirement under the Uganda Traffic and Road Safety Act 

- Vehicle number plate and at least one vehicle photo 

- Proof of insurance (even basic third-party cover) 

- Emergency contact 

⚠ A driving permit is mandatory for the Car Student Agent track. UniRush cannot waive this requirement as it is a statutory legal obligation, not a platform policy. Students without a permit are advised to apply through UNRA before activating the car role. 

## **3.4 Role Switching — How It Works** 

A student with an active Agent role sees a Role Switcher at the top of the home screen: 

- CLIENT MODE: standard experience — browse, order, track deliveries 

- AGENT MODE: rider/driver experience — go online, accept jobs, earn money 

The student cannot be in both modes simultaneously. Switching modes pauses any active activity on the other role (e.g. if the student is waiting for a delivery as a client, they cannot go online as a rider until that order is completed or cancelled). 

## **3.5 Student Agent Earning Rules** 

- Earnings are paid to the student's registered mobile money number 

- Student Agents on the Motorcycle track earn the same per-trip rate as professional boda riders within their permitted zone 

- Student Agents on the Car track earn the same rate as professional car owners 

- Minimum withdrawal: UGX 2,000 

- Earnings are visible in the Agent Wallet tab, separate from the Client wallet 

_Note: Student Agent accounts are reviewed at the start of each academic semester. Students who are no longer enrolled will have their Agent role suspended until re-enrollment is confirmed._ 

## **4. Client Experience: Placing an Order** 

Once registered, the client home screen shows four categories: Food & Drinks, Shopping, Transport, and Other Requests. 

## **4.1 Home Screen** 

- Search bar — find sellers or specific items 

- Category tiles: Food, Shopping, Transport, Other 

- Nearby sellers based on campus zone 

- Active order tracker (if an order is in progress) 

- Order history and repeat shortcuts 

- Role Switcher (visible only if the student has an active Agent role) 

## **4.2 Ordering Food or Shopping Items** 

**Step 1: Select Category —** Tap Food & Drinks or Shopping. 

**Step 2: Browse Sellers —** Listed with ratings, estimated delivery time, delivery fee, and open/closed status. 

**Step 3: Open a Seller —** View menu with prices in UGX, item photos, and stock status. 

**Step 4: Add to Cart —** Tap items to add quantities. Special instructions can be added per item. 

**Step 5: Review Cart —** See itemised total, delivery fee, and service fee. Apply promo code if available. 

**Step 6: Set Delivery Location —** Choose a saved location or pin a new point on the map. Rider instructions can be added. 

**Step 7: Place Order —** Order is sent to the seller for acceptance. 

**Step 8: Seller Confirmation —** Seller has 3 minutes to accept or the order is auto-cancelled **Step 9: Choose Payment Method after Seller Confirmation —** MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, UniRush Wallet, or Card. Payment is held in escrow. 

**Step 10: Live Tracking —** GPS tracking activates once a rider picks up the order. 

**Step 11: OTP Handoff —** Client receives a 4-digit OTP at delivery. Rider enters it to confirm receipt. Payment is released after OTP. 

**Step 12: Rate the Experience —** Client rates seller and rider separately out of 5 stars. 

## **4.3 Requesting Transport** 

**Step 1: Select Transport —** Tap Transport on the home screen. 

**Step 2: Choose Vehicle Type —** Boda for quick solo trips; Car for groups or longer distances. 

**Step 3: Set Pickup & Destination —** Estimated fare is shown before confirming. 

**Step 4: Confirm Booking —** Client sees driver name, photo, and plate before the trip begins. 

**Step 5: In-App Communication —** Messaging through the app only — no direct phone numbers shared. 

**Step 6: Completion —** Fare deducted on arrival. Client rates the driver. 

## **4.4 Raising a Dispute** 

- Tap Order History → Select order → Report Issue 

- Choose: Missing item, Wrong item, Damaged/tampered, Other 

- Upload a photo of the received package 

- Platform reviews within 24 hours; refund processed within 48 hours if upheld 

## **5. Seller Experience** 

Sellers  manage  everything  from  the  Seller  Dashboard  —  available  in  the  app  or  at sellers.unirush.ug. 

## **5.1 Dashboard Overview** 

- Store toggle: Open / Closed / Busy 

- Live incoming order panel with audio alerts 

- Menu management: add, edit, hide items 

- Sales summary: today, weekly, monthly 

- Ratings and reviews 

- Payout history and pending balance 

## **5.2 Receiving and Managing Orders** 

**Step 1: Order Alert —** Push notification shows: items, quantities, client delivery zone, estimated pickup time, and order value. 

**Step 2: Accept or Decline —** Seller has 3 minutes to respond. Declined orders require a reason. 

**Step 3: Prepare Order —** Seller prepares items and can update the estimated preparation time. 

**Step 4: Package —** All orders must be in sealed, tamper-evident packaging. Platform provides sticker kits. 

**Step 5: Mark Ready —** Seller taps Order Ready. The dispatch engine is notified to assign a rider. 

**Step 6: Rider Pickup —** Rider presents QR code. Seller scans it to confirm the correct order is collected. 

**Step 7: Handoff Confirmed —** Responsibility for the order passes to the rider after the scan. 

## **5.3 Seller Payments** 

- Daily payout at 8:00 AM for all previous day's confirmed orders 

- Payout via MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, or weekly bank transfer 

- Platform commission deducted per transaction per seller agreement 

- URA-compliant digital receipts generated for every transaction 

- Disputed orders are held pending resolution; non-disputed orders pay out normally 

## **6. Boda Rider Experience** 

Boda riders use a low-data Rider App optimised for Ugandan mobile network conditions. The interface is minimal to avoid distraction while riding. 

## **6.1 Going Online** 

- Tap Go Online — GPS location is confirmed 

- Must be within a registered delivery zone to receive jobs 

- Student Agents on the Motorcycle track are automatically restricted to their 3 km campus zone 

- Zone preference can be set (e.g. UCU zone, Mukono town zone) 

## **6.2 Receiving and Completing a Job** 

**Step 1: Job Alert —** Shows: seller location, distance, item type, estimated earnings. 60 seconds to accept. 

**Step 2: Accept —** Navigate to seller via in-app maps. 

**Step 3: Arrive & Scan QR —** Scan seller's QR code; verify seal is intact and item count matches. 

**Step 4: Picked Up —** Tap Picked Up. Navigation switches to client location. 

**Step 5: Deliver —** Navigate to client. Client location is only visible during active delivery. 

**Step 6: OTP Entry —** Enter 4-digit OTP provided by client to confirm delivery. 

**Step 7: Earnings Credited —** Per-trip earnings added to Agent Wallet immediately after OTP. 

## **6.3 Earnings & Withdrawals** 

- Withdraw any time to registered mobile money number 

- Minimum withdrawal: UGX 2,000 

- Earnings: base fee + distance fee + surge multiplier (if active) 

## **6.4 Safety Features** 

- SOS panic button — sends GPS location to platform emergency team and next of kin 

- In-app messaging only — client phone numbers are never exposed 

- Route deviation alerts if rider leaves expected corridor by more than 500 m 

- Platform micro-insurance covers riders per trip (in partnership with a registered Ugandan insurer) 

- 3-strike policy for professional riders; 2-strike policy for Student Agents (lower threshold due to limited zone) 

## **7. Car Owner / Driver Experience** 

Car owners operate in Transport Mode, handling passenger trips and bulk deliveries. Student Agents on the Car track access the same interface under the same restrictions as professional car owners, except that they are identified as Student Agent in their profile badge. 

## **7.1 Accepting a Passenger Trip** 

**Step 1: Trip Request —** Driver sees pickup, destination, estimated fare, and client rating. **Step 2: Accept & Navigate —** Client sees driver name, photo, plate, and live location. **Step 3: Confirm Client —** At pickup, driver verifies the client's name in the app matches the passenger. 

**Step 4: Trip in Progress —** Route is tracked. Communication via in-app chat only. 

**Step 5: Completion —** Driver taps Trip Complete. Fare auto-deducted. Client rates driver. 

## **7.2 Bulk Goods Delivery** 

- Bulk Delivery jobs show total weight, number of stops, and earnings per stop 

- Scan each parcel's QR before departure from the seller 

- Each drop point requires a separate OTP from the client at that stop 

- Per-stop earnings credited after each successful OTP

## **8. How Transportation Is Handled** 

## **8.1 Delivery Matching Logic** 

- When a seller marks an order Ready, the dispatch engine identifies online riders within 3 km 

- Ranked by: proximity → rating → completed trips → last active time 

- Job offered to top-ranked rider first; cascades every 60 seconds if not accepted 

- Student Agent riders are included in the matching pool but only for deliveries within their campus zone 

- If no rider accepts within 5 minutes, client and seller are notified with options to wait or cancel 

_Note: Peak surge pricing of 1.2x–1.5x applies during 12:00–2:00 PM and 6:00–9:00 PM to attract more riders online._ 

## **8.2 Passenger Ride Matching** 

- Car owners matched on proximity, vehicle suitability, and rating 

- Client can specify: Car only, Boda only, or Either 

- Scheduled rides pre-assigned to a driver who confirms 30 minutes before pickup 

- Student Agent cars appear in matching but are labelled Student Agent in the client's booking screen 

## **8.3 GPS & Live Tracking** 

- All active trips tracked in real time via device GPS 

- Client can see rider/driver on map during delivery/trip only 

- Tracking data is not stored beyond 72 hours (PDPA compliance) 

- Geofencing alerts fire if rider leaves expected corridor by more than 500 m 

- Student Agent riders who leave their 3 km campus zone during a job are flagged and the job is paused 

## **9. Payments & Financial Flow** 

## **9.1 Payment Flow** 

**Step 1: Client pays upfront —** Full amount (items + delivery + service fee) collected via mobile money or wallet. 

**Step 2: Escrow —** Funds held by the platform until OTP delivery confirmation. 

**Step 3: Delivery confirmed —** OTP entered; platform releases: seller share, rider share, and retains service fee. 

**Step 4: Dispute window —** Client has 30 minutes post-OTP to raise a dispute. **Step 5: Seller payout —** 8:00 AM daily for all previous day's confirmed orders. 

**Step 6: Rider payout —** Rider/Agent withdraws from wallet on demand. 

## **9.2 Accepted Payment Methods** 

- MTN Mobile Money (primary) 

- Airtel Money 

- UniRush Wallet (preloaded via mobile money top-up) 

- Visa / Mastercard (via payment gateway) 

## **9.3 Refund Policy** 

- Before seller accepts: full refund within 5 minutes 

- After seller accepts, before pickup: 80% refund (20% to seller for preparation) 

- After pickup: no refund (all parties fulfilled obligations) 

- Successful dispute: full or partial refund within 48 hours 

- Failed delivery due to rider fault: full refund 

## **10. Policies & User Agreements** 

Every user must read and accept the applicable policies during registration. Acceptance is recorded with a timestamp and the user's phone number as a binding digital acknowledgement. Policies are presented in plain language within the app and users must scroll through the full document before the acceptance checkbox becomes active. 

## **10.1 Universal Platform Policy (All Users)** 

Accepted by every user at registration regardless of role. 

**P1. Accurate Information:** You agree to provide accurate, current, and truthful information during registration and at all times while using the platform. Providing false information, including fake IDs, false business details, or impersonating another person, results in immediate permanent account suspension. 

**P2. One Account Per Person:** Each individual may hold only one UniRush account. Duplicate accounts are detected automatically and will be merged or the duplicate suspended. 

**P3. Lawful Use Only:** The platform may not be used to transport, purchase, or deliver prohibited items including but not limited to: controlled substances, counterfeit goods, firearms or ammunition, alcohol without a valid licence, or items stolen from others. 

**P4. Respect for All Users:** Harassment, threatening language, discrimination, or abusive behaviour towards any other platform user — whether in-app chat, during a delivery, or during a ride — is a zero-tolerance offence resulting in immediate suspension pending investigation. 

**P5. Privacy of Other Users:** You may not record, photograph, or share information about other users obtained through the platform without their consent. Client delivery addresses and phone numbers seen during a job are confidential and may not be stored or shared. 

**P6. Platform Integrity:** Attempting to manipulate ratings, fabricate completed deliveries, exploit refund policies fraudulently, or interfere with the dispatch system is grounds for immediate permanent suspension and potential referral to law enforcement. 

**P7. Governing Law:** This agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of Uganda. Disputes that cannot be resolved through the platform's internal process may be referred to the courts of Uganda. 

## **10.2 Client (Student) Policy** 

Accepted by all clients at registration, in addition to the Universal Platform Policy. 

**C1. Student Verification:** You confirm that the student registration number and access number you provide belong to you. The platform will verify these against university records where an integration exists. Providing another student's credentials is a violation of both this policy and the University's academic integrity rules. 

**C2. OTP Responsibility:** The 4-digit delivery OTP is personal to you. Do not share it with anyone until you have physically received and inspected your order. Entering the OTP before receiving the order waives your right to a dispute for that delivery. 

**C3. Accurate Delivery Location:** You are responsible for setting an accurate delivery location. Deliveries cannot be completed if the rider cannot locate you. Refunds will not be issued if a delivery fails because the client is unreachable at the given location. 

**C4. Dispute Honesty:** You agree not to raise false disputes. Raising a dispute claiming items are missing or damaged when they were properly delivered is fraud and will result in account suspension and recovery of any incorrectly refunded funds. 

**C5. Age Requirement:** You must be at least 18 years old or have parental/guardian consent to use the platform. Ordering alcohol (where legally listed) requires confirmation that you are 18 or older. 

**C6. Transport Safety:** When using transport services, you must board only the vehicle whose plate number matches what is displayed in the app. If the plate does not match, do not board and report the discrepancy immediately using the in-app SOS or Report button. 

## **10.3 Seller Policy** 

Accepted by all sellers at registration, in addition to the Universal Platform Policy. 

**S1. Legal Trading:** You confirm that your business is legally registered or operating under an applicable exemption, and that you hold all licences required to trade in your category (e.g. food hygiene certificate, alcohol licence where applicable) under Ugandan law. 

**S2. Product Accuracy:** All items listed on the platform must be accurately described and priced in UGX. Unavailable items must be removed or marked out of stock immediately. Clients who receive items materially different from the listing description are entitled to a full refund at the seller's cost. 

**S3. Packaging Standards:** All outgoing orders must be packed in sealed, tamper-evident packaging before handoff to a rider. Failure to seal orders properly that subsequently results in a client dispute will be resolved against the seller. 

**S4. Order Acceptance Time:** Sellers must accept or decline orders within 3 minutes of receipt. Consistent failure to respond within this window (more than 10% of orders in a 7-day period) may result in the seller being temporarily unlisted to protect client experience. 

**S5. Tax Compliance:** Sellers are responsible for their own tax obligations to URA. The platform provides digital receipts and transaction records to assist with filing. The platform does not act as a tax agent on the seller's behalf. 

**S6. Health & Safety:** Sellers handling food must maintain hygiene standards as required by the Uganda Food and Drugs Act and applicable KCCA regulations. The platform reserves the right to investigate complaints and temporarily suspend sellers pending a hygiene review. 

**S7. Commission Agreement:** By activating your seller account you agree to the platform commission rate communicated during onboarding. Rates are subject to change with 30 days written notice to the seller. 

## **10.4 Professional Rider & Driver Policy** 

Accepted by all professional boda riders and car owners, in addition to the Universal Platform Policy. 

**R1. Permit & Licence Currency:** You confirm that all permits, licences, and documents submitted during registration are valid and current. You must notify the platform immediately if any document expires or is suspended. Operating on an expired permit is a legal offence and a violation of this policy. 

**R2. Vehicle Roadworthiness:** You are responsible for ensuring your vehicle is in roadworthy condition at all times while active on the platform. The platform is not liable for accidents caused by vehicle defects. 

**R3. Package Integrity:** You must inspect every package at pickup. If a seal is broken or the package appears tampered with before you accept it, you must refuse to collect and report the issue through the app before leaving the seller. Once you confirm pickup, you assume responsibility for the package. 

**R4. Helmet Compliance:** Boda riders must wear a properly fitted helmet at all times while operating on the platform, as required by the Uganda Traffic and Road Safety Act Cap 361. Client complaints about helmet non-compliance will be treated as a policy violation. 

**R5. Passenger Safety (Drivers):** Car owners may not carry more passengers than the licensed capacity of the vehicle. Passengers must use seatbelts where fitted. Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs is an immediate permanent ban and will be reported to Uganda Police. 

**R6. OTP Requirement:** Deliveries must not be marked as complete without a valid OTP from the client. Marking a delivery complete without an OTP is fraud. 

**R7. Route Compliance:** Riders and drivers must follow the most direct reasonable route to the destination. Significant unexplained deviations will trigger a platform alert. Deliberate detours that increase client costs or delay delivery are a policy violation. 

**R8. Earnings & Tax:** You are an independent contractor on the UniRush platform, not an employee. You are responsible for any applicable income tax obligations on your earnings under the Uganda Income Tax Act. 

## **10.5 Student Agent Policy** 

Accepted by all students who activate the Boda or Car role, in addition to both the Universal Platform Policy and the Client Policy (as they remain a client on the same account). 

⚠ This policy contains important limitations specific to Student Agents. Please read it carefully before activating the Agent role. 

**SA1. Student Status Requirement:** You confirm that you are currently enrolled at the university stated during registration. Your Agent role is conditional on active enrollment. If you graduate, defer, or are suspended, your Agent role will be deactivated within one semester review cycle or upon the platform being notified, whichever comes first. 

**SA2. Vehicle Ownership or Permission:** You confirm that the motorcycle or car you have registered either belongs to you or that you have the owner's explicit permission to use it for commercial delivery purposes. The platform may ask for proof of this permission at any time. 

**SA3. Campus Zone Restriction (Motorcycle):** If you do not hold a valid riding permit, your delivery zone is strictly limited to within 3 km of your university's main gate. The platform enforces this automatically using GPS geofencing. Attempting to circumvent the geofence (e.g. by disabling location services mid-delivery) will suspend your Agent account immediately. 

**SA4. Driving Permit (Car):** Even as a Student Agent, a valid driving permit is legally required to operate a motor vehicle on Ugandan roads. The platform cannot and will not waive this requirement. Students who operate a car without a permit are in violation of the Uganda Traffic and Road Safety Act and this policy, and their account will be permanently suspended. 

**SA5. Academic Priority:** UniRush is a supplementary income source. The platform does not encourage students to compromise their academic work. The Student Agent role can be temporarily paused at any time by the student (e.g. during exams) from within the app without penalty. 

**SA6. Helmet Requirement:** All Student Agent boda riders must wear a helmet, regardless of whether they hold a permit. This is non-negotiable. 

**SA7. Incident Reporting:** In the event of an accident, theft, or any incident during a delivery or trip, you must report it through the in-app SOS or Incident Report tool within 1 hour of the incident. Failure to report incidents discovered later through client complaints will be treated as a concealment violation. 

**SA8. Upgrade Path:** Student Agents are encouraged to obtain a formal riding permit and upgrade to the Professional Rider tier. Upgrading removes zone restrictions and qualifies the rider for surge pricing jobs and long-distance deliveries. 

**SA9. Termination of Student Agent Role:** The platform reserves the right to deactivate the Student Agent role if the student accumulates 2 upheld complaints in any 30-day period, violates any clause of this policy, or is no longer enrolled at a recognised institution. The student's Client account remains active unless a separate Client policy violation also occurs. 

_By ticking 'I have read and agree to the UniRush Student Agent Policy' during registration, you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by all clauses above. Your phone number and the timestamp of this acceptance are recorded as your digital signature._

- All orders must be in sealed, tamper-evident packaging before rider pickup
