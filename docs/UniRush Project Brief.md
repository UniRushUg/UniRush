## **3. Short Description** 

UniRush  is a campus-focused delivery application that connects individuals who need items delivered with available delivery personnel — including boda boda riders, bicycle couriers, and fellow students. The platform streamlines on-campus and near-campus deliveries, offering a fast, reliable, and affordable service for the university community. 

## **4. Purpose of the Project** 

Currently, students and campus residents face significant challenges when trying to access goods and services from within and around campus. The most common workaround — contacting individual boda boda riders like a personal courier — is unreliable, inaccessible to those without the right contacts, and cannot scale to meet demand. 

UniRush addresses this by: 

- Providing a single, trusted platform to request and manage deliveries. 

- Giving independent riders and students a structured way to earn income through delivery work. 

- Eliminating the inconvenience of physically sourcing goods from across campus. 

- Building a safe, rated, and accountable delivery ecosystem within the university environment. 

## **5. Goals** 

## **5.1 Primary Goals** 

- Provide quick, reliable, on-demand delivery services within and around campus. 

- Create a safe and accountable transaction environment for senders and riders. 

- Offer a convenient alternative to physical pickup of goods. 

- Generate income-earning opportunities for idle students and boda boda riders. 

## **5.2 Secondary Goals** 

- Establish Send It as the go-to campus logistics solution. 

- Build a rating and trust system for delivery personnel. 

- Support local small businesses on and around campus through delivery partnerships. 

- Lay the foundation for potential expansion beyond the initial campus scope. 

## **5. Scope** 

## **5.1 Geographic Scope** 

The service is strictly limited to areas in, within, and immediately around the campus. 

## **5.2 Service Scope** 

UniRush  will support the following delivery categories: 

|**Service Category**|**Examples**|
|---|---|
|Package Delivery|Clothes, documents, academic materials, personal items|
|Food Delivery|Meals from campus cafeterias or nearby eateries|
|Grocery & Supermarket|Shopping runs from campus or nearby stores|
|Academic Errands|Printing, stationery, library book pickups|



## **5.3 User Scope** 

The application will support three types of users: 

- Customers — students, staff, or anyone on campus placing delivery requests. 

- Delivery Personnel — boda boda riders, bicyclists, or student couriers who sign up to fulfill deliveries. 

- Administrators — platform managers who oversee operations and user management. 

## **5.4 Out of Scope (Current Version)** 

- Deliveries outside the defined campus area. 

- International or inter-city logistics. 

- Real-time inventory management for vendors. 

## **6. Feasibility** 

## **6.1 Technical Feasibility** 

Applications of this nature have already been built and proven globally (e.g., Uber Eats, Glovo, Jumia Food). The technology stack required — mobile/web frontend, backend APIs, geolocation, and payment integration — is well-documented and accessible to a capable development team. 

## **6.2 Economic Feasibility** 

Similar delivery platforms have demonstrated strong revenue potential through commissionbased models, delivery fees, and business partnerships. At the campus level, lower 

infrastructure costs and a captive user base make this a financially viable venture from the outset. 

## **6.3 Legal & Political Feasibility** 

Delivery and logistics applications of this type operate legally and are not subject to special restrictions. The platform will comply with applicable local business, data protection, and labor laws. 

## **7. Functional Requirements** 

The following features must be present in the Send It application: 

## **7.1 User Registration & Authentication** 

- Users (customers and delivery personnel) can create accounts using email or phone number. 

- Delivery personnel must complete a verification process before going active (ID, profile photo). 

- Secure login with password and optional two-factor authentication. 

## **7.2 Delivery Request Management** 

- Customers can place a delivery request specifying pickup location, drop-off point, and package type. 

- Customers can select a delivery category (food, package, grocery, etc.). 

- Customers can view estimated delivery time and cost before confirming. 

- Orders can be tracked in real time from pickup to delivery. 

## **7.3 Delivery Personnel Features** 

- Riders/students can view available delivery requests near them and accept or decline. 

- Delivery personnel can set their availability status (online/offline). 

- Riders can view their delivery history and earnings summary. 

## **7.4 Payment System** 

- Customers can pay via mobile money, cash on delivery, or in-app wallet. 

- Delivery fees are automatically calculated based on distance and package type. 

- Delivery personnel receive earnings tracked in-app with withdrawal options. 

## **7.5 Rating & Review System** 

- After each delivery, customers can rate their delivery person (1–5 stars) and leave feedback. 

- Delivery personnel can also rate customers. 

- Ratings are visible on user profiles to build trust. 

## **7.6 Notifications** 

- Push notifications for order confirmation, rider assignment, pickup, and delivery completion. 

- SMS fallback for users without data access. 

## **7.7 Admin Dashboard** 

- Admins can manage and monitor all active deliveries. 

- Admins can approve, suspend, or remove delivery personnel accounts. 

- Platform analytics: total deliveries, revenue, active users, and performance metrics.
